Don't let your eyes fool you.

I drift across the sky, a formless fluffernutter, Made of a million tiny dreams, not a drop of butter. I hold the whispers of forgotten lullabies, And the forgotten squeaks of a thousand rubber duckies. The wind tickles my belly, and I morph and churn, A giant celestial churner, forever unburned. I weep diamonds at times, on a schedule erratic, Drenching unsuspecting picnickers with a glitter attack. What am I?
